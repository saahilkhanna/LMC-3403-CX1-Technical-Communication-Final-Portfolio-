Major Project 3

Georgia Tech Library Toolkit

Project Overview

For this group project, we developed resources for the Georgia Tech Library, creating materials that help students discover and effectively use key library services. Our team identified a core problem: while the Library offers valuable resources like printing zones, technology lending, and research databases, this information is often spread across multiple web pages and students typically learn about these services through word of mouth. This also usually happens when they're already under time pressure.

Working with Enya Gu, Mokshith Reddy Mannuru, and Aditya Devarapalli, we designed two complementary deliverables intended for placement in residence halls and high-traffic campus spaces. These materials introduce library services before students arrive at the building and include QR codes for immediate, detailed access. This project demonstrates our ability to conduct audience analysis, create user-centered instructional materials, and collaborate effectively as a technical communication team.

Design Rationale

Why We Created This Toolkit

Hard to Find

Information spread across multiple web pages that students rarely explore unless they already know what they're looking for.

Last-Minute Discovery

Students usually learn about printing, equipment lending, or study spaces through word of mouth, often under time pressure.

Confusing Terminology

Instructions assume familiarity with campus systems or terminology (like "printing zones") that newer students don't have.

Process

How We Collaborated

We decided to split the workload by deliverable while still coordinating closely through a group chat to keep our tone and design consistent across both pieces. Mokshith and I worked on the "Library Access and Navigation Guide" while Enya and Aditya took on "Technical Communication Using the Georgia Tech Library."

My Role in Deliverable 2

Because I have more experience with Canva, I took the lead on the visual design of the poster. I created the layout, organized the sections, and arranged text so that it was easy to skim and visually clear. I also generated the QR codes that direct students to relevant library links, making the poster interactive and actionable.

Content Division

Mokshith and I divided the content into four main sections. I drafted and refined the text for the "Printing and Computing Resources" section and the "Private Study and Collaboration Spaces" section while Mokshith took on the other two. We worked in person together so it was easy to critique and respond to each other's suggestions.

Overcoming Challenges

Our main challenge was scheduling, we all have different class and activity schedules. We addressed this by relying heavily on digital communication: sharing screenshots, PDFs, and drafts over text, and leaving detailed comments so others could respond asynchronously.

Iteration

Responding to Feedback

We made several minor but meaningful changes to our deliverables' appearance and content based on peer and instructor feedback:

Removed unnecessary arrows pointing to the QR codes in Deliverable 2, since they cluttered the design.
Expanded the printing section to explain zone information more clearly and added details about what can be found in each zone.
Revised our recommendation report because we had originally assumed audience familiarity with the assignment, we expanded on why GT Library's current communication is lacking.

The rest of the feedback from students was generally positive and reflected that they had faced the same issues themselves and would have appreciated having something like this earlier.

Looking Forward

Future Recommendations

Website Development

Create a dedicated website with video tutorials showing how to conduct effective research using library databases, archives, and more. Include guides on effective searching using keywords and qualifiers.

Regular Updates

Frequent updates should be made to ensure spaces and articles referenced are up-to-date. QR codes need to be refreshed and maintained, ensuring they are always accessible.

Outreach Events

Host awareness events during the beginning of semesters when new/transfer students are common, seminars, Week of Welcome activities, and lectures with library staff.

Key Highlights

Audience Analysis

Our primary audience was current and incoming undergraduates who may feel overwhelmed by unfamiliar terminology. We avoided jargon and explained campus-specific terms in straightforward language while maintaining professional accuracy for secondary audiences like library staff.

Rhetorical Strategy

We organized deliverables around student needs in specific moments, rather than just listing everything the Library offers. We focused on problems students face: discovering resources at the last minute, under time pressure, through word of mouth.

Practical Placement

These materials are designed for residence halls and high-traffic campus spaces, introducing library services before students arrive at the building. QR codes let students immediately pull detailed instructions onto their phones.

Career Relevance

This project asked me to think like a communicator, not just a student. I had to connect design, genre, and audience needs into one coherent strategy, skills directly applicable to my future career in technical communication.

Skills Demonstrated

Key Takeaway

Effective communication is about timing and context as much as content. Putting the right information in the right format, in the right place, can matter just as much as what the message actually says.
