Major Project 1

Career Portfolio

Project Overview

For this project, I developed a comprehensive career portfolio targeting Software Engineering internship positions. The assignment challenged me to create professional job materials, a tailored resume and cover letter, and a portfolio website while demonstrating technical communication excellence through audience analysis, iterative revision, and professional design.

My materials were specifically crafted with the tech industry in mind. I emphasized the quality of my experiences and reasons that make me hireable, throughout the process. This project illustrates my ability to adapt messaging for specific audiences, respond constructively to feedback, and present myself professionally across multiple communication modes. Lets see how below!

Key Feedback Received

The primary feedback was to tailor the cover letter to a specific company rather than keeping it generic. The original version used placeholders like "[Company Name]" and "[Hiring Manager's Name]," which weakened the rhetorical impact and personal connection with the reader.

Final Version
Download

Changes Made

Addressed the company specificity by targeting the cover letter to a real position. Replaced generic placeholders with actual company details, demonstrating audience awareness and genuine interest in the role. Also refined the overall professional tone and formatting.

Key Highlights

Rhetorical Awareness

Tailored my cover letter specifically for Software Engineering internship positions, adapting my messaging to resonate with recruiters and hiring managers. Emphasized transferable skills from my Equifax experience and Georgia Tech research that directly align with industry expectations.

Iterative Process

Responded to instructor feedback about making the cover letter specific to a real company. The original version used generic placeholders, which weakened its rhetorical impact. I revised it to target an actual position, demonstrating how specificity strengthens audience engagement and shows genuine interest.

Multimodal Approach

Combined written materials (resume and cover letter) with this digital portfolio website to create a cohesive professional presence. Integrated proper business letter formatting, professional document design, and web-based presentation for maximum accessibility.

Design Decisions

Created a clean, scannable resume layout that allows recruiters to quickly identify key qualifications. Applied business letter formatting to my cover letter following Purdue OWL guidelines, ensuring professional presentation that meets industry standards.
